Strategic Parterner



Founded in 2011 by Mary McKenna, she has been joined by a dynamic operational team. They are supported by an advisory board who are leaders in the fields of education and language development.

Vision

We believe that access to early literacy education shouldn't be based on economic circumstance. When you partner with us, you ensure that teachers around the world have the tools they need to teach literacy in their classrooms, and can provide their students with access to endless opportunity.


Mission

The National Educational Support Trust, USA, called NEST, is a nonprofit organization committed to serving teachers and students with the most up to date research based language and literacy materials. Our projects reach classrooms worldwide through the use of onsite volunteers and a web based curriculum delivery system called WordScientists.org, designed to scale up our success on a global scale.



Briding Futures will continue to work with NEST: Literacy for Peace and Justice so as to spread literacy and the
access to education throughout India in the coming years.

Why Top Parent was started?

90% of a child’s brain develops before the age of 5. Ensuring continuous learning and providing the right stimulus is critical in these years. The pandemic unfortunately marginalizes millions of children from low-income communities who are first generation learners.

A young child’s brain develops in response to the relationships and experiences around it in the early years. With no access to schools or tuition and lack of any support at homes, we needed a bold solution, i.e. Top Parent.



Welcome to bridging Futures

What We Do?

Developed by NudgEd, with funding support from the Central Square Foundation - a non-profit organization working to improve the quality of school education - ‘Top Parent’ offers a curriculum based on the NCERT pattern for children aged between 3-8 years.

The mobile application is a one-stop free solution that provides parents with strategies and resources in simple languages to help them support their child’s learning and well-being during these tough times.
